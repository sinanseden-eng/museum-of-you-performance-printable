Brochure Pages 2, 3, and 4: Exhibit Highlights

Select three specific items from your life (e.g., a skateboard, a ticket, a hoodie). For each, write a Speculative Description guessing how historians in the year 3000 will interpret it.

⚠️ Grammar Requirement: You must use modals of speculation (must have, might be, can't have) for each item!
Example: "This object produced loud noises. Future historians might think it was a weapon. It must have required great skill to hold, but it can't have been comfortable."

Brochure Pages 5-6

Back Cover: Curator's Philosophy

This is where you show deep critical thinking. Write 2-3 detailed paragraphs answering the questions below to form your Curator's Philosophy.

  1. The Ultimate Artifact: If this museum burns down in a thousand years, and archaeologists can only save ONE of your artifacts, which one should it be? What does that single item teach the future about what the youth of the 21st century truly valued?
  2. Perspective & Bias: To what extent does perspective and bias influence whether a single artifact can be considered reliable evidence of the values of 21st-century youth?
  3. The Curator's Responsibility: How does the act of selecting and preserving knowledge shape representation, and what responsibilities come with deciding what future generations will know about us?

Final Deliverable

Time to build your museum! Take the blank A4 paper given to you by your teacher and fold it carefully into three equal sections (a tri-fold). Once folded, use this drafting guide to transfer your written text, layout your sketches, and design the final beautiful brochure for The Museum of You.
